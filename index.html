Did you know that currently, the rate of extinction is 1000-10000 times higher than the normal rate?

Some of the world's most endangered animals

The Amur Leopard, number 2 on the list, with only around 100 left in the wild. They can only be found in small parts of Russia and China, and face issues like habitat loss due to transport infrastructure and prey scarcity.
The Sumatran Tiger is number 3 on the list. This is largely due to the massive spike in population in SEA, leading to more poaching and habitat loss.
Mountain Gorillas, number 4 on the list. As the name suggests, they live in the mountainous areas of Congo and Uganda. Currently, there are just over 1000 of them in the wild
Black Rhinoceros, number 7 on the list. Largely hunted for their horns as trophies and for trade, their losses have more than doubled since the 1990s.
